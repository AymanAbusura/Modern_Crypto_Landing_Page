Emerging in 2009 as an open-source innovation, Bitcoin wasn't just the first cryptocurrency; it introduced a paradigm shift in financial control. While initially undervalued, trading for a mere fraction of a cent, the growing recognition of Bitcoin's ability to preserve value during economic turmoil has propelled its adoption and demonstrated its power as a safeguard in an increasingly uncertain world. Its design creates a limited supply and is not at all susceptible to bad governance which devalues traditional currency.
